Momentum

Momentum is a measure of how powerfully something is moving.

Heavier objects will have more momentum than lighter ones, and objects that are moving faster will have more momentum than those that move slowly.

When two objects collide with each other (no, please don't try this with your friends in the skatepark!), the object with the greater momentum gets to continue moving in the same direction.

When objects are rolling or sliding down a path, the objects with greater momenta will travel farther than those with less.

How Does This Apply to Skateboarding?

The distance you can roll on your skateboard is partially determined by your momentum. You can roll farther when you have more momentum.

What Determines Momentum?

Momentum is determined by this formula:

P = mv

In this formula, P is momentum, measured in kilogram meters per second. The variable m represents mass in kilograms, and the variable v represents a positive velocity in meters per second.

Put simply, momentum is just mass times speed. That means that, if an object's mass is zero or it isn't moving, its momentum is zero, since zero times anything is zero. If one object has twice as much mass as another and they are both moving at the same speed, the object with twice as much mass will have twice as much momentum. If an object with the same mass as another object is moving at twice the velocity, it will also have twice as much momentum.

How Much Momentum Do YOU Have?

You can easily use the momentum formula above to figure out your momentum by entering some information in the Momentum Calculator on this page.

You will need to know your mass and velocity. Mass, as mentioned in the Potential to Kinetic Energy Page, is very different from weight because it is always the same, regardless of the strength of the gravitational field. Your weight will change if you stand on another planet, but your mass will stay the same. Your weight on earth can, however, be used to discover your mass. The Weight-To-Mass calculator on this page will allow you to easily do this.

But how do you find your velocity? This part is much trickier. You may have to estimate your speed for now. Later on, however, we plan to enable this website to access the accelerometers and gyroscopes inside your phone so that you can see your momentum and other data live as you ride your skateboard.
