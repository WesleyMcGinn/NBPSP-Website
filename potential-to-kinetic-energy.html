Potential to Kinetic Energy

Potential Energy is the energy that something has within it that can allow it to move later on. This energy is stored, like in a stretched rubber band.

Kinetic Energy is energy that creates movement.

In order for anything to move, its potential energy must be converted to kinetic energy. For example, when a stretched rubber band is released, the potential energy that it once held is immediately converted to kinetic energy, which thrusts its movement. This conversion from potential to kinetic energy is what this page is all about!

How Does This Apply to Skateboarding?

When you are at the top of a ramp, you have the potential energy to go down. When you roll over the side of the ramp, this potential energy is converted to kinetic energy.

With a little bit of physics, you can determine exactly how much potential gravitational energy you have at the top of the ramp and how much kinetic energy you will have once you reach the bottom.

And here's the best part: When you know how much kinetic energy you have at the bottom of the ramp, you can find out exactly how fast you will be moving!

Finding Your Potential Energy

This equation is used to determine potential gravitational energy:

GE = mgh

With the potential energy calculator on this page, you can easily use this formula to determine your potential gravitational energy at the top of the ramp!

First, you will need to find your mass. Mass is very different from weight. Unlike weight, mass doesn't change in different gravitational fields. But you can find out what your mass is from how much you weigh on Earth. The Weight-to-Mass calculator on this page will easily allow you to do this.

Finding Your Kinetic Energy and Speed

Since ALL of your potential energy will be converted to kinetic energy when you go down the ramp, your kinetic energy at the bottom of the ramp will be EQUAL to your potential gravitational energy at the top of the ramp.
